Signs You Need a Social Media Strategy

Many businesses in Kenya are active on social media, but few are strategic. If you’re unsure whether your organisation needs a formal strategy, here are clear signs that you’re operating without the direction needed to see real returns:

1. Inconsistent Posting and Messaging

If your platforms have long gaps between posts, or if each department posts with a different voice, your brand appears disjointed. This inconsistency erodes trust and reduces your visibility across algorithm-driven feeds.

Example: A Nairobi-based e-commerce startup posted heavily during product launches but remained inactive for weeks after. With a clear content strategy and calendar in place, they achieved a 130% increase in reach within two months of regular, strategic posting.

2. Low Engagement Despite Frequent Activity

Posting often but receiving little engagement is a sign your content isnt aligned with audience interests or platform behaviours. A strategy helps identify what content works, when to post, and how to encourage interaction.

Stat: In Kenya, posts published between 11am and 2pm on weekdays show up to 40% higher engagement (Meta Business Insights, East Africa, 2024).

3. No Link Between Social Media and Sales or Leads

If youre spending time or money on social without clear lead generation, traffic, or sales attribution, its time to rethink your approach. A proper strategy connects social outputs to business KPIs like conversions, inquiries, or downloads.

4. Unclear Audience Targeting

Are you speaking to everyone and reaching no one? A good strategy segments your audienceby industry, age, interest, or behaviourand matches them with the right platforms, content, and messaging.

5. Disjointed Campaigns and Missed Opportunities

Without planning, you miss seasonal opportunities, trending conversations, or campaign synergy across platforms. Strategy enables long-term planning, campaign integration, and real-time adaptability.

6. Team Confusion or Burnout

Your team is constantly rushing to create content, lacks clear roles, or repeats past mistakes. A social media strategy creates workflows, approvals, templates, and performance benchmarks to streamline execution.

If any of these signs sound familiar, its time to move from guesswork to growth.
