What is SEO?

SEO is the practice of strategically optimising your website to improve its ranking in search engine results pages (SERPs) for searches that are relevant to your target audience in Kenya. The goal is to make your website appear at the top of search results when Kenyans look for products, services, or information related to your niche.

How Do Search Engines Work?

Imagine a huge library containing all the information in the world. Search engines are like the librarians of this digital library, constantly working to categorise and organise websites so users can easily find what they’re looking for. Here’s a peek into how search engines work:

  • Crawling: Search engines employ special software programs called bots, often referred to as “crawlers” or “spiders.” These bots continuously search the internet, discovering new websites and following links between them, just like a librarian would catalogue new books in a library.
  • Indexing: Once a crawler discovers your website, it analyses its content and structure. This process is similar to a librarian creating a record for a new book in the library’s catalogue. The indexed information helps search engines understand what your website is about and how it might be relevant to user search queries.
  • Ranking: This is where the real magic happens! Search engines use complex algorithms, like a librarian using a sophisticated filing system, to determine which websites are most relevant and valuable for a particular search query. These algorithms consider various factors, including the content on your website, its user experience, and how other websites link to you. The higher your website ranks in the search results, the more likely it is for Kenyans to find you when they search online.

How do Search Engines Work?

Search engines like Google, Yahoo! etc. function through a three-stage process: crawling, indexing, and ranking. Let’s get into the specifics of each stage to understand how websites end up on Kenyan search engine results pages (SERPs).

Stage 1: Crawling

Search engines employ software programs called crawlers, or spiders, to navigate the wide world web. These crawlers systematically discover new web pages and follow links between them to map out the information.

  • Discovery: Crawlers rely on sitemaps and existing links to find new web pages. They also revisit known URLs periodically to check for updates.
  • Data Fetching: Once a crawler discovers a webpage, it downloads the content, including text, code, and images.
  • Prioritisation: Crawlers prioritise crawling based on various factors, including website freshness, update frequency, and crawl depth limitations set for each site. This ensures efficient use of resources and avoids overwhelming a website with crawl requests.

Stage 2: Indexing

After the crawlers have gathered the raw data, it’s time for organisation. Search engines build a colossal index, essentially a digital library containing information about every crawled webpage. This index structures and stores the data for efficient retrieval during user searches.

  • Content Processing: The downloaded webpage content goes through a parsing process. Here, the search engine analyses the text, HTML structure, and metadata to understand the webpage’s subject matter and thematic relevance.
  • Link Analysis: Links act as citations within the digital library. Search engines record the links found on each webpage, building a vast web of connections that helps them understand the relationships between different websites and assess a webpage’s authority.

Stage 3: Ranking

When a Kenyan user types a search query, the search engine doesn’t simply search the indexed data. It employs complex algorithms, and sophisticated mathematical formulas, to rank the most relevant web pages for that specific query. Here’s where the true magic happens:

  • Keyword Matching: The algorithm identifies keywords within the user’s query and searches for web pages within the index that contain those keywords. However, it goes beyond simple keyword stuffing. Modern algorithms consider synonyms, semantic relationships, and the overall context of the webpage content.
  • Relevance Ranking: This is where the real power lies. The algorithm goes beyond just keyword presence. It considers a multitude of factors to determine which web pages are most relevant and valuable to the user’s search intent. These factors include:
    • On-page Optimisation: Content quality, keyword placement within the content, title tags, meta descriptions, and website structure all play a role.
    • Off-page Optimisation: The quality and quantity of backlinks a webpage receives from other reputable websites significantly influence its ranking.
    • User Engagement Metrics: Factors like bounce rate, time spent on the page, and click-through rate from search results pages (SERPs) indicate user engagement and can influence ranking.
    • Algorithmic Updates: Search engine algorithms are constantly evolving to improve their accuracy and effectiveness. Staying updated on these changes is crucial for maintaining a strong SEO presence.

The final output is a ranked list of web pages displayed on the SERPs for the user’s query. The higher a webpage ranks, the more likely it is for Kenyan users to discover it and fulfil their search intent.

Keywords in SEO

What are Keywords?

Keywords are the building blocks of SEO success in Kenya. They represent the search terms users type into search engines like Google to find information, products, or services. Effective keyword research allows you to understand what Kenyans are actively searching for online and strategically optimise your website’s content to resonate with those searches.

Types of Keywords

  • Head Keywords: These are broad, high-volume search terms with intense competition (e.g., “mobile phones”). They offer a glimpse into overarching industry trends but can be difficult to rank for, especially for new websites.
  • Mid-Tail Keywords: Offering a balance between search volume and competition, mid-tail keywords are more specific than head keywords but still encompass a broad range of user intent (e.g., “best mobile phones under KES 10,000”).
  • Long-Tail Keywords: Highly specific and often phrased as questions (e.g., “Where to buy a refurbished iPhone in Nairobi”), long-tail keywords have lower search volume but boast higher conversion rates because they target users with clear purchase intent.

Finding the Sweet Spot

The ideal keyword strategy incorporates a mix of head, mid-tail, and long-tail keywords relevant to your Kenyan audience. This approach ensures you cater to users at various stages of the buying journey, from those browsing for general information to those ready to make a purchase.

Keyword Research

Keyword research is the cornerstone of creating a data-driven SEO strategy. Here’s a step-by-step approach to effective keyword research for the Kenyan market:

  1. Seed Keyword Brainstorming: Identify a small set of broad keywords related to your niche and Kenyan target audience. Consider what products or services you offer and what Kenyans might search for.
  2. Leveraging Search Engine Tools: Utilise free and paid tools to discover related keywords, search volume estimates, and competition levels. Some prominent options include:
    • Google Keyword Planner: This free tool by Google provides insights into search volume, competition level, and related keywords. It’s a fantastic starting point for initial research.
    • SEMrush: A powerful SEO suite offering advanced keyword research features, including keyword difficulty score and local search volume data for Kenya. (Paid plans available)
    • Ahrefs: Another comprehensive SEO toolkit with in-depth keyword research capabilities, including competitor keyword analysis. (Paid plans available)
  3. Analysing the Competition: Use SEO tools to discover what keywords your Kenyan competitors rank for. This can provide valuable insights into industry trends and potential keyword gaps you can exploit.
  4. Prioritising Keywords: Not all keywords are created equal. Consider factors like search volume, competition level, and relevance to your target audience when prioritising keywords to target.

On-page SEO focuses on optimising the elements within your website that search engines can directly crawl and index.
