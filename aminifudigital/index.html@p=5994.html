What Are Google Shopping Ads?

Definition of Shopping Ads / Product Listing Ads (PLAs)
Google Shopping Adsalso known as Product Listing Ads (PLAs)are visually rich, product-focused adverts that display key product elements: image, title, price, store name, and ratings. These ads surface products directly in search results or other platforms, enabling highintent shoppers to spot, click and buy in one seamless experience. Google Shopping Ads Services in Kenya put your products in front of ready-to-buy customers across Google Search, the Shopping tab, YouTube and the Display Network.

How they differ from standard Google Search Ads
Unlike text ads that rely solely on keywords, Shopping Ads pull structured product data from your feed. This means Google decides when to show your ad based on relevance and data qualitynot just your bidding strategy or copy. The ad unit includes visuals and essential product information, giving instant clarity to a buyer. In short: Shopping Ads sell products, not ideas.

Where they appear

  • Google Search results (the main SERP product carousel)

  • Dedicated Google Shopping tab

  • Google’s Display Networkincluding retargeted placements

  • Within YouTube Shopping features

Key elements
A typical Shopping Ad comprises:

  • Image of the product (crucial for mobile-first Kenyan shoppers)

  • Product titleoptimised for search terms

  • Price in local currency (KES where applicable)

  • Store namebuilds brand recall

  • Ratings/reviewswhen availableboost user trust and CTR

Supporting data
In Kenya, Google Shopping Ads deliver an average click-through rate (CTR) of approximately 2.30%, with a remarkably low average cost-per-click (CPC) of just 0.06 (roughly KES 12), offering cost-effective visibility in a growing e-commerce space.
Globally, Shopping Ads dominate retail search: they account for 76% of retail search ad spend and attract 85% of all clicks in Google Ads campaigns.

Why Shopping Ads Matter for E-commerce Brands in Kenya

With Google Shopping Ads Services in Kenya, brands reach high-intent buyers at the top of Google results. For Kenyan e-commerce businesses, visibility and relevance are everything. With mobile penetration exceeding 64% and internet penetration at over 42% in 2025, the average Kenyan consumer is spending more time searching and shopping online than ever before. Google Shopping Ads place your products directly in front of buyers who are not just browsing, but actively looking to purchase.

Targeting High-Intent Buyers

The biggest strength of Shopping Ads lies in their ability to reach people at the moment of purchase intent. When a user types in buy iPhone 14 Nairobi or womens leather handbag Kenya, theyre not seeking general information, theyre looking for a supplier. Shopping Ads use structured product data from your Google Merchant Center feed to match these search terms precisely with relevant products. This precision targeting ensures your budget is spent on clicks that have a high likelihood of converting.

Higher CTR Through Visual Engagement

Shopping Ads consistently outperform text-based search ads when it comes to click-through rates (CTR). The combination of product images, prices, store names, and ratings creates a richer and more persuasive first impression. In Kenya, CTRs for Shopping Ads average around 2.30%, often double the engagement of equivalent text ads. This is particularly impactful in retail sectors like fashion, electronics, and beauty, where visuals strongly influence buying decisions.

Prime Placement at the Top of Search Results

Shopping Ads appear above organic results and even above most paid text ads in Google Search. This premium placement ensures your products are one of the first things a potential buyer sees. For mobile-first Kenyan shoppers, where mobile devices account for over 90% of search traffic, this positioning dramatically increases the chances of click-through and purchase.

Reaching the Mobile-First Kenyan Shopper

Kenyas online shoppers are heavily mobile-driven. The visual nature of Shopping Ads is perfectly suited for small screens, where concise and compelling information is essential. The product image immediately captures attention, while the displayed price and store name help the user decide whether to click without having to read lengthy descriptions.

Level Playing Field Against Larger Retailers

Competing with established chains or international brands can feel impossible through traditional advertising. Shopping Ads allow smaller Kenyan e-commerce businesses to compete head-to-head by focusing on highly relevant search terms, optimised product feeds, and strategic bidding. A well-optimised Shopping campaign can outperform bigger competitors that rely solely on brand recognition.

Competitive Advantage in Emerging E-commerce Categories

While sectors like electronics and fashion dominate Kenyan Shopping Ads, emerging categories such as home dcor, niche health products, and artisanal goods are gaining traction. Early adoption of Shopping Ads in less saturated product categories allows brands to establish dominance before competition intensifies.

The bottom line: Shopping Ads are a high-ROI channel for Kenyan e-commerce brands ready to convert intent into sales. Businesses that invest in optimising their Merchant Center feed, targeting the right queries, and continuously refining their campaigns can achieve significant growth in both traffic and revenue.

Our Shopping Ads Services Include

Our Google Shopping Ads Services in Kenya cover setup, feed optimisation, campaigns, tracking, and scaling. amini Digital Agency provides a complete Google Shopping Ads management service for Kenyan e-commerce businesses. We handle the technical setup, feed optimisation, campaign structuring, and ongoing optimisation so your products consistently appear in front of ready-to-buy customers. Each stage of our service is designed to improve product visibility, click-through rate, and return on ad spend.

Google Merchant Center Setup

Google Merchant Center (GMC) is the foundation of any Shopping Ads campaign. Without it, your products cannot be listed on Google Shopping. We start by creating and verifying your Merchant Center account, ensuring that it is correctly linked to your website and Google Ads account. This includes:

  • Domain verification through DNS, HTML file upload, or Google Analytics.

  • Business information setup, including your store name, branding assets, and contact details.

  • Shipping and tax settings configured according to Kenyan delivery zones and rates.

  • Policy compliance checks to ensure your return and refund pages meet Google requirements.

A properly configured GMC account reduces the risk of product disapprovals and campaign delays.

Product Feed Creation and Optimisation

The product feed is a structured file that contains details about each product in your catalogue. Google uses this data to determine which searches will trigger your ads. We optimise this feed to maximise relevance and compliance, including:

  • Crafting clear, keyword-rich product titles that match search intent in Kenya.

  • Writing concise but detailed descriptions that highlight product benefits and specifications.

  • Adding correct Google product categories, GTINs, and other attributes.

  • Ensuring all images meet Googles quality guidelines and load quickly on mobile.

  • Using feed rules to correct errors and ensure consistency in naming, pricing, and availability.

An optimised feed directly impacts your impression share and click-through rate. Feed quality is the backbone of effective Google Shopping Ads Services in Kenya.

Campaign Setup and Structuring

Once the feed is ready, we design your Shopping Ads campaigns to align with your sales objectives and budget. Our approach includes:

  • Selecting between Standard Shopping campaigns for more manual control or Performance Max (formerly Smart Shopping) for automated targeting and bidding.

  • Segmenting products by category, price range, margin, or performance to apply tailored bids.

  • Choosing the right bid strategy, whether manual CPC for control, target ROAS for profitability, or maximise clicks for traffic growth.

  • Adding negative keywords to prevent ads from appearing for irrelevant searches.

This structure allows for efficient budget allocation and clearer performance insights.

Tracking and Analytics

Measuring performance is as important as running the campaign itself. We set up advanced tracking through Google Analytics 4 (GA4) and Google Ads conversion tracking. This allows us to monitor:

  • Click-through rate (CTR)

  • Cost per click (CPC)

  • Return on ad spend (ROAS)

  • Impression share compared to competitors

We also provide dashboards that show real-time campaign performance so you can see exactly how your budget is being used.

Ongoing Management and Optimisation

Shopping Ads require regular adjustments to maintain and improve results. Our ongoing management includes:

  • Updating the product feed when new items are added or stock changes.

  • Testing product images, titles, and pricing strategies to improve CTR.

  • Adjusting bids based on product performance, location, and device type.

  • Scaling budgets for products that deliver strong sales while reducing spend on underperforming items.

  • Adding seasonal or promotional campaigns to capitalise on peak shopping periods such as Black Friday or back-to-school seasons.

This continuous optimisation keeps your campaigns competitive and aligned with market trends in Kenya.

Requirements to Run Shopping Ads

Before your products can appear in Google Shopping results, there are specific technical, compliance, and operational requirements you must meet. These are not optional; failure to meet them will result in product disapprovals, account suspension, or poor campaign performance.

1. An Active E-commerce Website with Product Pages

Google requires a functional, publicly accessible website that clearly lists the products you want to advertise.
Your site should have:

  • Dedicated product pages with unique URLs for each item.

  • Detailed product descriptions, specifications, and clear pricing in Kenyan Shillings (KES).

  • High-quality product images that load quickly and meet Googles image standards (minimum 100100 pixels for non-apparel products, 250250 pixels for apparel).

  • Accurate stock availability so buyers do not click on items that are sold out.

Googles automated crawlers will scan your website to confirm that the product data in your Merchant Center feed matches what is displayed on the site.

2. Verified Google Merchant Center Account

You must have a Google Merchant Center (GMC) account linked to your Google Ads account.
Key steps include:

  • Domain verification via HTML file upload, meta tag, Google Analytics, or Google Tag Manager.

  • Business information setup including store name, logo, and contact details.

  • Shipping and delivery settings configured for Kenyan locations and international markets if applicable.

  • Tax settings (while Kenya has no e-commerce-specific sales tax entry in Merchant Center, you must still ensure prices include VAT where applicable).

Without verification, your feed cannot be approved, and no Shopping Ads can run.

3. Compliant Product Feed

The product feed is the structured file (XML, CSV, or Google Sheets) that contains your product information.
To meet Googles feed requirements:

  • Include all mandatory attributes such as id, title, description, link, image_link, price, availability, and condition.

  • Add recommended attributes like GTIN (barcode number), MPN, and Google product category for better matching.

  • Use clear and relevant keywords in product titles and descriptions to match buyer search terms.

  • Ensure that feed data matches your live website content exactly. Any price, availability, or currency mismatches can lead to disapprovals.

  • Remove prohibited items such as counterfeit goods, dangerous products, or restricted health items.

4. Approved Return, Refund, and Contact Policies

Google requires visible, accessible policies on your website that explain:

  • How customers can return products and within what timeframe.

  • Conditions for refunds and how they are processed.

  • Contact options such as a phone number, email, or contact form.

These pages build trust and are mandatory for policy compliance.

5. Linked Google Ads Account

Your Merchant Center account must be linked to your Google Ads account to run campaigns. This connection allows you to create Shopping campaigns using the products in your feed.
Once linked, you can segment products, set bids, apply targeting, and measure performance from the Google Ads interface.

6. Operational Readiness

Even with a fully compliant setup, success depends on operational factors:

  • Reliable fulfilment to ensure timely deliveries.

  • Accurate inventory updates to prevent overselling.

  • Customer service availability to respond to pre- and post-sale queries quickly.

A technically correct campaign can still underperform if the customer experience is poor after the click.

    FAQs Shopping Ads in Kenya

    Can Shopping Ads work for my Shopify store?

    Yes. Shopify connects to Google Merchant Center through the official Google & YouTube app, which syncs your catalogue automatically. We still optimise the feed in Merchant Center to improve search relevance. That includes tighter product titles, clearer descriptions, correct categories, image checks, and feed rules to fix missing or inconsistent data. We then link Merchant Center to Google Ads, confirm conversion tracking in GA4, and apply product segmentation so your budget favours bestsellers and higher-margin items.

    What is the difference between Standard Shopping and Smart Shopping (Performance Max)?

    Standard Shopping gives you granular control. You can segment by category or margin, set bids at product group level, and add negative keywords to cut waste. Performance Max automates placements across Search, Shopping, Display, YouTube, Discover, and Gmail, and uses data signals and conversion history to push for sales. Our default approach in Kenya is a hybrid. Keep high-value or seasonal products in Standard Shopping for precision and learning, and use Performance Max to expand reach once the feed and tracking are strong.

    How long does it take to see results?

    If Merchant Center approves the feed quickly, expect traffic within 1 to 2 weeks. Algorithmic optimisation typically stabilises by weeks 4 to 6. Timelines depend on catalogue size, competition, and data quality. We front-load work on feed quality, conversion tracking, and campaign structure so early spend produces reliable signals. That shortens the path to profitable bids and clearer scaling decisions.

    What budget should I start with?

    A practical starting range for Kenyan e-commerce is KES 50,000 to KES 100,000 per month. This funds testing across product groups and devices while gathering enough data for statistically sound decisions. If unit margins are thin or competition is high, begin at the lower end, protect margin with tighter segmentation, and scale once target ROAS is reached. We reallocate budget weekly to back proven products and reduce exposure on underperformers.

    Can I run Shopping Ads if I sell internationally?

    Yes. Configure additional target countries in Merchant Center, submit prices in the correct currency, and set country-specific shipping. Ensure language and policy pages meet local rules. We recommend separate campaigns or Performance Max asset groups per market so bids, creatives, and budgets align with local demand and competition. Inventory accuracy is critical when stock is shared across regions.

    What happens if my products get disapproved?

    Disapprovals usually come from missing attributes, price or availability mismatches, poor images, or policy issues. We monitor Merchant Center diagnostics daily, correct the feed with rules or attribute fixes, and align website data with feed values. If a policy error is incorrect, we submit an appeal with evidence. Preventative maintenance matters. Schema, consistent pricing, clear variants, and clean images reduce future disapprovals and keep impression share steady.

    Can I advertise seasonal or limited-edition products?

    Yes. Create dedicated campaigns or product groups for seasonal or limited lines. Increase bids during peak demand and cap spend when stock runs low. Keep stock and availability current in the feed to avoid overselling. For short windows such as Black Friday or back to school, schedule feed fetches more frequently, refresh images and prices, and layer promotions to lift click-through and conversion rate.
